My Art Journey
Hand Fabrication

While attempting to finish a degree in English Literature I took an Intro to Jewelry class on a whim to fulfill an elective credit. I loved the class and the professor. Because of that I spent more time on my jewelry class than any other. The professor suggested I consider metal arts as a new direction to take my studies and the rest as they say is history.

Digital Jewelry Design

I started digitally designing for a wedding ring manufacturer in my final year of college to help pay for school and a study abroad program. I enjoyed the flexibility that designing within a computer program gave me. I wasn't out the cost of metal if my idea didn't work, I could simply adjust the program. After graduating that job turned full time because I enjoyed it so much.

Fluid Art

This is where my journey might not seem to match. Jewelry is surprisingly rigid with the rules ivolved in getting a piece of jewelry to be wearable. I needed a creative outlet where the final result mattered far less than the process. Fluid art caught my attention for those very reasons. It's messy with no strict rules and it sure is fun!
